Magnet Arm Alignment

Pushed Spin

The Project:

I found an old Popular Mechanics book with a great many projects for young kids. I immediately felt awful about myself after reading (again, in a book targeted towards those half my age) about all the plans for wind powered snow sleds and motorized rafts that I won’t be able to make in my wildest dreams (just kidding, the second I have money, full send). I did find instructions (as good as 40 year old instructions can be) on how to make a toy motor with a nail, a tin can, and some enamel wire. I ended up cutting out a slice of 1/8th inch sheet metal for my arm, and made a neat little wood base. I found some magnet/enamel wire on Amazon and did my best. It doesn’t work at all like it’s supposed to, but it was able to magnetize and was a fun project to see a motor work at its most basic and to construct one from scratch. I hope to continue exploring motors and winding my own, and maybe even make one work one day.

-December 2025

PS: Those burn marks are not from me being wire dumb (though I am) but are from when I burned off enamel cover from the wires, and thought burning the wood too would look cool... I swear!

At a Glance:

  • Built wooden base out of tree round and wood
  • Filed nail and attached arm to act as rotating axle
  • Bent metal “u” and wound enamel wire to serve as electromagnet
  • Successfully powered system with external power supply
  • System was put together properly but was too imprecise preventing full spinning
